# Drachenlord Topic Map

This file groups the dossier into future quiz categories, topic clusters, and tag families.

## Why this file exists

The raw dossier is rich but too broad to use directly as a question-writing guide.
This topic map helps with:
- consistent category naming
- balanced question selection
- difficulty planning
- separating safe core material from specialist or caution-only material

## Working rules

- Prefer broad, reusable categories over very narrow one-off buckets.
- Keep category names understandable for players, even if internal tags are more technical.
- Use tags to capture nuance; use categories to control game flow.
- Keep high-volume topics balanced so one cluster does not crowd out the rest of the quiz pool.

## Proposed category set

### 1. Identity & Basics
- **Scope:** name, alias, place, core identifying facts, very basic orientation
- **Use for:** easy entry questions and onboarding rounds
- **Typical source areas:** BG-001, BG-004, PA-001, QZ-001
- **Suggested tags:** alias, identity, place, basics, creator
- **Good difficulty mix:** mostly easy, some medium
- **Avoid:** private personal details; scene gossip
- **Example facts:** Drachenlord alias; Altschauerberg; Drachenschanze meaning

### 2. Timeline & Milestones
- **Scope:** major years, turning points, chronology, escalation markers
- **Use for:** ordering questions, year recall, event sequencing
- **Typical source areas:** T-001 to T-015, PH-001 to PH-006, EK-003, EK-008
- **Suggested tags:** timeline, year, milestone, escalation, chronology
- **Good difficulty mix:** easy to hard
- **Avoid:** tiny scene-internal incidents without wider relevance
- **Example facts:** channel start; 2014 address release; 2018 Schanzenfest; 2022 channel removal

### 3. Media & Documentation
- **Scope:** documentaries, podcasts, public reporting, major media framing moments
- **Use for:** media recognition and outside-perception questions
- **Typical source areas:** T-005, T-014, EK-002, EK-010, ME-001 to ME-006, M-003
- **Suggested tags:** documentary, podcast, y-kollektiv, cui-bono, media
- **Good difficulty mix:** medium to hard
- **Avoid:** highly fragmented or unverified uploads and reuploads as direct answer sources
- **Example facts:** Y-Kollektiv 2016; Cui Bono 2022

### 4. Legal & Regulation
- **Scope:** authorities, court decisions, sentencing changes, media regulation
- **Use for:** serious milestone questions and harder fact recall
- **Typical source areas:** T-004, T-008, T-009, T-010, EK-001, EK-004, EK-006
- **Suggested tags:** court, appeal, sentence, blm, regulation, law
- **Good difficulty mix:** medium to hard
- **Avoid:** legal interpretation beyond what the dossier clearly states
- **Example facts:** BLM action in 2019; first-instance sentence; appeal outcome

### 5. Scene Terms & Dynamics
- **Scope:** defined terms, structural dynamics, recurring concepts, analytical vocabulary
- **Use for:** concept questions, matching questions, definition rounds
- **Typical source areas:** BG-002 to BG-006, KM-001 to KM-006, KR-001 to KR-008
- **Suggested tags:** terminology, dynamic, anti-fandom, swatting, scene-language
- **Good difficulty mix:** medium
- **Avoid:** mocking meme language unless it has analytical value and is clearly framed
- **Example facts:** Drachengame meaning; swatting; Drachenschanze; anti-fandom

### 6. Place & Infrastructure
- **Scope:** location, house symbolism, archives, platform loss, infrastructure manipulation
- **Use for:** questions about place, preservation, and the shift from online material to archive logic
- **Typical source areas:** EK-008, EK-009, EK-012, AM-001 to AM-003, INF-001 to INF-003, LV-001
- **Suggested tags:** place, archive, deletion, youtube, maps, infrastructure
- **Good difficulty mix:** medium to hard
- **Avoid:** over-reliance on archive objects that are only weakly proven
- **Example facts:** house demolition; YouTube channel deletion; Google Maps manipulation

### 7. Media Manipulation & Disinformation
- **Scope:** false attribution cases, fake interview episodes, misinformation spillover
- **Use for:** specialist rounds or higher-difficulty question sets
- **Typical source areas:** AK-004, AK-005, EK-005, EK-007, EK-011, ME-007
- **Suggested tags:** disinformation, fake-interview, kongsberg, utrecht, media-failure
- **Good difficulty mix:** mostly hard
- **Avoid:** using these as baseline easy questions
- **Example facts:** Utrecht false attribution; Kongsberg false attribution; fake Bild interview

### 8. Wider Debate & Comparative Context
- **Scope:** Maskengame framing, NWO relation, broader public debate, analytical expansion beyond the single case
- **Use for:** advanced rounds and thematic comparison sets
- **Typical source areas:** T-015, BG-006, VP-001 to VP-003, MA-008, V-002, V-003
- **Suggested tags:** maskengame, comparison, law, politics, broader-context
- **Good difficulty mix:** hard
- **Avoid:** direct linkage claims that the dossier itself treats as uncertain
- **Example facts:** Maskengame as umbrella debate term; NWO as separate but related phenomenon

## Recommended category balance for future question pools

To avoid one topic drowning out the others, use a rough balancing rule for general mixed pools:

- Identity & Basics: 10–15%
- Timeline & Milestones: 20–25%
- Media & Documentation: 15–20%
- Legal & Regulation: 15–20%
- Scene Terms & Dynamics: 10–15%
- Place & Infrastructure: 10–15%
- Media Manipulation & Disinformation: 5–10%
- Wider Debate & Comparative Context: 5–10%

This is only a working guideline and should be adapted once the real question volume grows.

## Suggested writing pipeline

1. Pick a category from this file.
2. Select a candidate fact from `drachenlord-fact-pool.md`.
3. Re-check the cited dossier anchors.
4. Draft the question in simple player-facing language.
5. Add tags that help future balancing.
6. Only then move the question into real seed data or API import format.

## Seed-writing guardrails

- Prefer facts that are clear outside the bubble.
- Prefer questions with one unambiguous answer.
- Avoid writing questions that require scene allegiance or insider jargon.
- Avoid trick questions based on unstable archive details.
- Prefer public-interest framing over humiliation framing.
